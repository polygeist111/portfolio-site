Lantern Model - 2024

Made for ASU's CPI 321 course, this lantern model was designed as part of a larger Game Art portfolio. The lantern was modelled in Blender, and is rendered here on the site with ThreeJS. In the versions in the following section displaying the full portfolio, rendering was done in Adobe 3ds Max. This model presented many challenges, chiefly the twisted wrought-iron posts. It turns out the Blender twist modifier doesn't work on rectangular prisms, but it works perfectly on four-sided cylinders. Oh, the joys of data types.

Hordekeep Art Portfolio - 2024

Made for ASU's CPI 321 course, this video portfolio contains six works of concept and process art for the imagined dark cozy game Hordekeep. Intended to display proficiency in a variety of software tools and digital mediums, the portfolio contains 2D, 3D, hybrid, and animated assets and art. Photoshop, Illustrator, Blender, 3ds Max, MaxMSP, OpenToonz, and After Effects were all used in the making of the portfolio. Additionally, it covers environmental, character, and prop art. Though I definitely have my favorites, I loved getting to use (and sometimes learn for the first time) these many different tools. The lantern in particular offered many valuable lessons, e.g. whatever you do, check your bevel settings. The model is many things, but optimized certainly isn't one of them. :)
